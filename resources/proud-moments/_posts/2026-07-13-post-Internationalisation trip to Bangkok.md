<p>Day 1: We tried all “Four Flavors” of Thai food at lunch. Then we dived
into Museum Siam to understand what “thai-ness” is. We ended the day making
our own keychains conducted by the Klong Bang Luang Community, a historical
community along the canal.</p>
<p>Day 2: We spent the day immersed in their culture and learning about their
school. Students even pulled off a performance for them! We also tried
the khim, a traditional Thai hammered dulcimer, and made luk chup desserts
by hand.</p>
<p>Day 3: Said goodbyes to our buddies at Matthayom Watnairong after exploring
their elective classes. Then we visited the Grand Palace and Temple of
the Emerald Buddha, the spiritual heart of Thailand and center of the Chakri
Dynasty. We ended the night at Nopparat Thai Classical Dance and Restaurant
with dinner and a cultural performance.</p>
<p>Day 4: Ayutthaya day</p>
<p>We explored Ayutthaya Historical Park, the former capital of Siam and
a UNESCO World Heritage Site, walking through Wat Mahathat, Wat Ratchaburana,
and Wat Phra Si Sanphet. Later we visited San Bao Gong Temple, one of the
oldest temples, and stood before its massive seated Buddha with roots in
early Chinese trade. So much history in one day!</p>
